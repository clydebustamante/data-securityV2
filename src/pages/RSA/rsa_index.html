What is RSA Cipher?

RSA is a popular encryption method used to keep digital information secure. It uses two keys: a public key, which is shared openly, and a private key, which is kept secret. The public key is used for encrypting messages, and the private key is used for decrypting them. The security of RSA is based on the difficulty of factoring large numbers, making it a reliable way to protect online communication, transactions, and digital signatures.
