What is Columnar transposition?

Columnar transposition is a cryptographic technique used for encrypting or rearranging information in a way that enhances security. In this method, the plaintext is written out in rows of a fixed length, and the columns are then rearranged based on a specific key. The resulting ciphertext is generated by reading the columns sequentially, creating a scrambled version of the original message. Decryption involves rearranging the columns back to their original order using the same key, allowing the recovery of the original plaintext. Columnar transposition provides a simple yet effective way to obfuscate information, adding an additional layer of complexity to protect sensitive data.
